Everything you need to practise

From casual song learners to professional musicians — Advanced Improviser gives you studio-grade loop tools in your pocket.

Click-Free Seamless Looping

Equal-power crossfade (10–15 ms) at every loop boundary. No pops, no glitches — just smooth, artifact-free playback powered by a native C++ Oboe engine.

Interactive Waveform Editor

10 zoom levels from 1/256× to 8×. Drag the waveform to scroll, tap to seek, and see a live time ruler that adapts to your zoom.

Unlimited Named Loop Regions

Create as many colour-coded regions as you like. Each has a custom name, precise sample-accurate boundaries, and drag handles with oversized 72 px touch targets.

Tempo Control

Slow down or speed up any track from 0.5× to 2.0× without changing pitch. Snap points at every 0.1× increment make it easy to lock in the right speed for your practice session.

Pitch Shift ±12 Semitones

Transpose any audio file up or down a full octave in either direction while keeping the tempo exactly where you want it. Perfect for transposing to a different key.

Auto-Save Loop Regions

Regions are saved automatically as lightweight JSON sidecar files keyed by audio content hash — not file name. Move or rename your file and your loops still load.

Handles Large Files Effortlessly

Memory-optimised engine streams and caches audio intelligently. Load files over 200 MB without crashes, with smart waveform downsampling for recordings over 30 minutes.

All Major Audio Formats

Import MP3, WAV, FLAC, M4A / AAC, and OGG files from anywhere on your device. Hardware-accelerated decoding keeps loading fast even on large files.

Recent Files & Persistent State

Reopen recent tracks instantly. Tempo, pitch, zoom level, and selected region are restored exactly as you left them — per file.

How it works

Three simple steps to a better practice session.

  1. 01

    Open any audio file

    Tap Select File and pick an MP3, WAV, FLAC, M4A, or OGG from your device storage. The app decodes it, renders the waveform, and loads any regions you saved previously.

  2. 02

    Create and name your loop regions

    Press Set Start at the beginning of the phrase, play a bit, then press Set End. Drag the handles to fine-tune. Add a name, pick a colour, and lock the handles so nothing moves by accident.

  3. 03

    Practice with tempo & pitch

    Start playback and dial in your tempo — slow it right down to 0.5× while learning a difficult passage, then ramp it back to 1× as you get comfortable. Shift the pitch if you need a different key.
